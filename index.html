Factor 1: Integrated land planning

What are some factors affecting the land dedicated for housing?


1. Limited Land Supply:

The suitable land for housing is limited due to presence of physical features like seas and mountains. The small land areas found in cities only adds on to the issue of limtied land supply.


2. Competition for Land Use:

There are many uses for land like residential, commercial, industrial and recreational uses as well as for infrastructures. This results in stiff competition for land use and insufficient land being set aside for housing. Additionally, land dedicated for one use will not be available for another use.


3. Different Perspectives:

Conflict of interests arise between various groups of people as there are different economic, social and environmental needs


These stakeholders come together to decide on the development of cities and manage different activites sustainably

The Main Stakeholders (only inclusive of the ones that Ms Loh said are important):


1. Singapore Land Authority (SLA):

-SLA manages land resources, maintains land records, and facilitates land transactions.
-They play a key role in land allocation and lease management.


2. Housing Development Board (HDB):

- HDB is in charge of making homes affordable and safe for all income groups.
- They plan, build, and manage public housing neighborhoods with provision of basic needs with the the inclusion of providing amenities and communities
- They also work with stakeholders to make sure these neighborhoods fit well into the city's plans
- Their framework includes the integration of the provision of basic needs as well as the inclusion of providing amenities and communities, promoting quality of living such as convenience and connectivity, encouraging social inclusivity within the neighbourhood


3. Transport and Infrastructure Authorities (e.g., Land Transport Authority):

- They plan and coordinate transportation and infrastructure projects to support the land-use plan, ensuring connectivity and accessibility.
- Their focus is on ensuring connectivity and accessibility in the city's transportation network
- Transport and Infrastructure Authorities enhance mobility, reduce congestion, and promote economic growth by facilitating efficient transport and infrastructure systems in the city
-more connectivity -> more people walking -> less cars on road -> less congestion -> less carbon emission


4. Developers and Property Owners:

- Private developers and property owners play a crucial role in proposing and executing development projects.
- They submit development proposals, seek permits, and invest in real estate projects.


5. Foreign Investors and Multinational Corporations:

- Foreign investors and multinational corporations may invest in real estate and influence land-use decisions through their projects and investments


6. Residents and Community Groups:

- Residents and community groups provide feedback on development plans and participate in public consultations.
- They advocate for community interests and help shape the direction of land-use policies.
