Factor 2: Why is inclusive housing important?

Why is inclusive housing important?


Inclusive housing is important to cater to different groups including:

- races

- Household sizes (multi-generational)

- Access to essential services for young parents, elderly

- Foster interaction

Provision of public housing


"Public housing refers to government efforts to provide housing units with basic services, usually for low-income households and other persons of need such as the elderly and disabled."


Public housing can be offered in the following ways:

1. Old flats that can be rented at a low price

2. Abandoned buildings that are converted for residential use

3. New apartments are built


Characteristics of inclusive housing:

1. Provides all residents with adequate housing

2. Provides all residents with access to all basic services such as transportation and recreation

3. All residents, regardless of income level or age, feel a sense of belonging and actively contribute to the community

4. Common places for people to interact and develop friendships, bonds and ties

Sub Factor 1: Affordable housing


Affordable housing is housing that is priced at a reasonable cost, such that residents still have enough money for other basic needs


Variety of housing types in Singapore cater to different income groups


Singapore hopes to become an inclusive city by providing inclusive housing



Different types of public housing meet the different needs of residents:

1. The primary purpose of building different flat types is to ensure that housing policies are flexible and adaptive to the changing demographics

2. Studio Apartments (catered to seniors)

3. 3-room flats (catered to small families)



*Financial schemes are available to subsidise cost of flats:*

1. Additional CPF Housing Grant (AHG)

2. Introduced in 2006 to help low-income families buy their first flat

3. Families earning not more than S$5,000 a month can qualify for a grant of up to S$40,000 which is used to pay for the flat



The Housing Development Board (HDB) plays a central role in providing affordable and quality housing for residents



Criteria: Housing Subsidy Options


1. HDB Subsidies:

-Additional CPF Housing Grant (AHG) and Special CPF Housing Grant (SHG) are means-tested subsidies.

-Aimed at lower- to middle-income households, they assist in purchasing an HDB flat.


2. Rental Housing Assistance:

-HDB provides subsidized rental housing options for those unable to afford home ownership.

-Heavily subsidized rental flats ensure affordability for low-income families.


3. CPF Housing Grants:

-Central Provident Fund (CPF) Housing Grants offer financial assistance.

-Grants like the Family Grant, Half-Housing Grant, and Proximity Housing Grant help eligible buyers offset HDB flat purchase costs.


*4. Elderly Housing Options*:

-Housing designed for elderly residents includes Studio Apartments (SAs) and 2-room Flexi flats. (Size)

-These options cater to the unique needs of the elderly and often come at more affordable rates. (Money)


5. Singles Housing Options (35 years and above)

-For singles aged 35 and above, HDB offers various housing options, including Studio Apartments (SAs) and 2-room Flexi flat

-These options are designed to cater to the housing needs of single individuals in this age group. (Based on single income)
