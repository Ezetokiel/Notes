Factor 3: Environmental Features in Buildings

What is a green building?

It's an integrated process that focuses on the relationship between the built-in environement and the natural environment (Green building video)

- reduced energy and water use

- healthy indoor environmental quality

- smart material selection


*Case study: HDB Grove Precinct*


*What environmental features are there?

1. Energy Efficient Lighting

- Energy-efficient lighting uses special bulbs that use less electricity to produce light.

- It reduces electricity use for lighting, which saves energy and lowers electricity bills.

- It's good for the environment because it reduces the demand for electricity from power plants, which can be polluting.


2. Double Glazed Windows

- Double-glazed windows have two layers of glass with a gap in between.

- They keep the heat out in hot weather

- They help save energy by reducing the need for air conditioning, making homes more comfortable and eco-friendly.


3. Solar Panels

- big, flat, shiny panels that turn sunlight into electricity

- provide clean energy for homes, reducing the need to use fossil fuels that contribute to air pollution

- help fight climate change, and save money on electricity bills

Case study: Pilot Solar-Ready Roof completed at Punggol Edge, Singapore


4. Green Roofs

- Green roofs are special roofs covered with plants and grass

- they keep buildings cooler, reduce energy use, and make cities greener

- Green roofs save energy, look nice, and help the environment

Case study: Block 756A Bedok Resovior View, Singapore


5. Rainwater Harvesting

- rainwater is collected from roofs stored and is used for things like watering plants and flushing toilets

- saves tap water, which is important becuase Singapore has limited natural water resources (reduces reliance on imported water

- rainwater harvesting helps save water and is good for the environment


Always link back environmental features to the alternative use of energy -> less fossil fuels being used -> lesser carbon footprint
